# Question-Research Mapping: Kompass Digitaler Wandel

Structured analysis of 35 topics and 198 German reflection questions against research findings on school digital transformation (April 2026). This document identifies research alignment, gaps, and opportunities for the Navigator Digitaler Wandel.

---

## Part A: Question-Research Alignment

### DIMENSION 1: PERSONEN/KOMPETENZEN (People & Skills)

#### Topic 1: Personale/soziale Kompetenzen (Personal/Social Skills)
**Questions:** 9 items covering the 4Ks (creativity, critical thinking, collaboration, communication), learning orientation, error culture, change management, collaboration, diversity of views, self-organization.

**Strong research backing:**
- Questions on "4K vorleben" and "Lernende sein" align directly with **Self-Determination Theory** (Chiu et al., 2024) — modeling autonomy and competence for learners.
- Error culture questions ("Wie lernen wir, Fehler als etwas Sinnvolles zu verstehen") reflect **Growth Mindset literature** (implicit in Pietsch 2024 on teacher digital mindset as distinct from skills).
- Change management questions ("Wie gehen wir mit Veränderungen um?") echo **Gkrimpizi et al.'s barrier taxonomy** — psychological readiness is a teacher-level barrier.
- Self-organization question ties to **TPACK-adjacent competencies** — teachers need metacognitive skills to adapt practice.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions on collaboration and diversity of views are fundamental but underspecified — they don't address the **perspective-switching insight** from Kampylis et al. (2023) that leaders and teachers rate capacity differently. A sharpened version could ask: "Wie nehmen verschiedene Berufsgruppen (LP, SL, PICTS, Eltern) unsere digitale Entwicklung unterschiedlich wahr?"

**Unique/original:**
- The framing of "Lernende sein" (being a learner oneself) is distinctly reflective and non-judgmental — not explicitly demanded by DigCompEdu frameworks but deeply aligned with the Navigator's pedagogical philosophy.

---

#### Topic 2: Fachkompetenz Medien Informatik (Professional Skills in Media & CS)
**Questions:** 3 items on building subject knowledge, support structures, roles of leadership.

**Strong research backing:**
- All three questions map directly to **teacher-level competency barriers** (Gkrimpizi et al., 2023). Content knowledge is a foundational barrier; support and role clarity reduce structural barriers.
- Reflects **TPACK's subject matter knowledge (SMK) component** — essential for effective technology integration.
- Leadership roles question aligns with **digital leadership literature** (implicit in Timotheou et al., 2022) — leaders must identify and empower subject experts.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions focus on *building* knowledge but not on *assessing existing gaps* or **professionalization pathways**. Could deepen with: "Welche Unterschiede in Fachkompetenz gibt es im Team? Wie unterstützen wir stärker und schwächer kompetente Personen auf ihrem Weg?"

**Unique/original:**
- The question about roles (SL, PICTS, etc.) is pragmatic and context-aware — doesn't assume a single model.

---

#### Topic 3: Fachdidaktik Medien Informatik (Specialised Didactics in Media & CS)
**Questions:** 4 items on pedagogy of CS/media, cross-disciplinary connections, methods, support structures.

**Strong research backing:**
- Questions on Lehrplan 21 competencies reflect **Swiss curriculum alignment** — necessary but not research-driven per se.
- "Fächerübergreifende Bezüge" aligns with **SAMR model** (substitution → redefinition across subjects) and **instructional design literature**.
- Methods question ("Wie gestalten wir den MI-Unterricht anregend und zielführend?") echoes **active learning and constructivism** — well-established in pedagogy.
- Support/training question again reflects teacher-level barriers.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions assume schools are already *implementing* MI curriculum but don't surface **what barriers prevent uptake**. Could be sharpened to map to Gkrimpizi's framework: "Welche Hindernisse (personaler, schulischer, systemischer Art) erschweren die Umsetzung des MI-Lehrplans?"

**Unique/original:**
- The integration of cross-disciplinary thinking is pragmatic and avoids siloing CS/media as standalone subjects.

---

#### Topic 4: Mediendidaktik (Media Didactics)
**Questions:** 3 items on embedding digital media in learning arrangements, adopting a "media lens," support structures.

**Strong research backing:**
- "Medienbrille" (media lens) is a **German-language pedagogical concept** aligned with **critical media literacy** — embedded in German curriculum and research.
- Questions on didactical embedding reflect **TPACK's pedagogical knowledge (PK) and technological pedagogical knowledge (TPK)** — how to teach *with* technology, not just *about* it.
- "Sinnvoll eingebettet" language avoids technology-for-technology's sake — philosophically aligned with **authentic task design** literature.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions don't explicitly address **AI-augmented media** — a gap since Ng et al. (2023) and Tenberga et al. (2024) show generative AI redefining "media" in education. Could extend: "Wie integrieren wir neue Formen von KI-generierten Medien didaktisch sinnvoll?"

**Unique/original:**
- The "Medienbrille" framing is distinctive and more reflective than checklist-based frameworks.

---

#### Topic 5: Anwendungskompetenzen LP (Application Skills — Teachers)
**Questions:** 4 items on skills needed for daily work, ensuring universal competency, strengthening/expanding skills, support structures.

**Strong research backing:**
- Universal competency question ("Wie stellen wir sicher, dass alle verfügen?") addresses **equity and the digital divide** — if teachers lack basic skills, digital access doesn't close gaps (Warschauer, 2004 framework).
- "Neuere Technologien" suggests **ongoing professional learning** — critical since tech changes faster than formal training cycles.
- Support structures question again reflects teacher-level barriers (Gkrimpizi et al., 2023).

**Partial backing:**
- Questions focus on *what skills* but not on **professionalization trajectories or peer learning networks**. Research shows peer-led professional learning (PLCs) outperform top-down training (implicit in Castano-Munoz et al., 2021). Could sharpen: "Wie nutzen wir interne Expertise und kollegiales Lernen, um Anwendungskompetenzen zu stärken?"

**Unique/original:**
- The distinction between "daily work" skills (application) and subject knowledge (Topic 2) and didactics (Topic 3) is pedagogically sound and avoids redundancy.

---

#### Topic 6: Haltungen (Mindsets)
**Questions:** 7 items on openness/curiosity/flexibility/motivation/innovation/persistence, values preservation, reflection, team diversity, concept of good teaching, digital risks/opportunities, transformation of understanding.

**Strong research backing:**
- Questions on mindset dimensions (openness, innovation, persistence) directly reflect **Pietsch (2024) on teacher digital mindset as distinct from skills** — a cutting-edge insight that Kompass captures well.
- Questions on values preservation ("Welche Werte sind uns wichtig?") echo **purpose-driven change management** literature — transformation without losing identity.
- Risk/opportunity framing reflects **critical perspective** on digital adoption — not techno-optimism but balanced assessment (aligned with Gkrimpizi's barrier framing).
- "Verändert sich unser Lern-, Bildungs- und Rollenverständnis?" is a profound reflective prompt aligned with **epistemological shifts in digital pedagogy**.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions don't explicitly address **technology acceptance and adoption psychology** (e.g., Rogers' Innovation Diffusion Theory, Davis' TAM). Could deepen: "Wer in unserem Team sind die Early Adopters, wer die Skeptiker? Wie nutzen wir diese Vielfalt konstruktiv?"
- No question on **digital well-being** or sustainable transformation — only rules/media education addressed later (Topic 14). Could add: "Wie schützen wir vor digitaler Überlastung und Burnout im Zuge der Transformation?"

**Unique/original:**
- The constellation of mindset questions is the Kompass's strongest differentiator from SELFIE — it probes psychological readiness and values rather than just measuring capability.
- The question on role change ("Rollenverständnis durch digitalen Wandel") is philosophically mature.

---

#### Topic 7: Zusammenarbeit mit Eltern (Parent Participation)
**Questions:** 5 items on family-school partnership, involving parents in development, home digital use/rules, explaining school philosophy, working with multilingual families.

**Strong research backing:**
- Family involvement question ("Wie arbeiten wir mit den Eltern zusammen?") maps to **parental digital literacy and shared responsibility** — critical for digital equity (Warschauer, 2004; van de Werfhorst, 2022).
- Home media use question reflects **out-of-school digital contexts** — increasingly important since students learn across formal/informal boundaries.
- School philosophy communication aligns with **stakeholder alignment and buy-in** (implicit in Gkrimpizi's school-level barriers).
- Multilingual families question is **equity-conscious** — acknowledges Swiss diversity and language barriers to engagement.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions don't address **digital divide at the family level** (van de Werfhorst's three levels: access, skills, outcomes). Could sharpen: "Wie unterstützen wir Familien mit unterschiedlichem Zugang zu digitalen Geräten und unterschiedlichen digitalen Kompetenzen?"
- No explicit mention of **counter-narratives to techno-optimism** — some parents have legitimate concerns (media addiction, privacy, surveillance). Could acknowledge: "Wie gehen wir mit Ängsten und Bedenken von Eltern bezüglich digitaler Medien um?"

**Unique/original:**
- The question on multilingual families is forward-thinking and equity-aware — rarely seen in digital transformation frameworks.

### DIMENSION 2: UNTERRICHT (Teaching & Learning)

#### Topic 8: Lernkultur (Learning Culture)
**Questions:** 6 items on definition of learning, understanding of education, continuous improvement, learner responsibility, changing role of teacher/learner, feedback culture.

**Strong research backing:**
- Questions on understanding learning and education are **foundational to pedagogy** — reflect constructivist vs. transmissionist paradigms. The prompt "Wie ändert sich dieses Verständnis angesichts des digitalen Wandels?" is sophisticated.
- Continuous improvement question aligns with **quality assurance and reflective practice** — core to school development.
- Learner responsibility question echoes **Self-Determination Theory** (Chiu et al., 2024) — autonomy drives motivation.
- Changing teacher role ("Wie verändert sich die Rolle der Lehrperson...da das gesamte Wissen jederzeit abrufbar ist?") directly addresses **epistemological disruption** caused by universal access to information — a profound insight not always explicitly named in frameworks.
- Feedback culture links to **formative assessment and learning sciences** — crucial for competency-based learning.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions don't address **AI-augmented learning** — with ChatGPT and generative AI, the question "knowledge is everywhere" becomes sharper and more urgent. Could extend: "Wie verändert sich unsere Rolle angesichts von KI, die Wissen nicht nur abrufen, sondern auch generieren kann?"
- No explicit mention of **learning analytics or data literacy** (Michos 2023) — schools increasingly have access to learning data but may not know how to interpret or use it. Could add: "Wie nutzen wir Daten über Lernprozesse, um unser Verständnis von Lernen zu vertiefen?"

**Unique/original:**
- The intellectual honesty of the knowledge-everywhere question is rare and sets a high bar for reflection.

#### Topic 9: (Über-)fachliche Kompetenzen (Interdisciplinary/Transversal Skills)
**Questions:** 5 items on labor market expectations, promoting 21st-century skills/4Ks, competencies supported by digital media, competency-oriented learning, motivation drivers.

**Strong research backing:**
- The 4K framework (creativity, critical thinking, communication, collaboration) is **well-established in 21st-century skills literature** — embedded in frameworks like OECD Learning Compass 2030.
- Questions on 4K promotion directly reflect **competency-based education** literature — a major shift from knowledge-based curricula.
- Digital media as competency enabler aligns with **SAMR and TPACK models** — technology as a tool for higher-order thinking.
- Motivation question ("Was fördert das «Wollen»?") ties to **Self-Determination Theory** (autonomy, competence, relatedness drive motivation) and **intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation** literature.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions assume consensus on "21st-century skills" but don't surface **divergent expectations** among stakeholders (teachers, employers, students, parents). Could sharpen: "Welche 4K-Kompetenzen erwarten Arbeitgeber, Eltern, und Schüler:innen von unseren Absolvent:innen — und wo sind die Unterschiede?"
- No question on **digital communication and collaboration skills** specifically — how students collaborate *through* digital media (vs. with media). Could add: "Wie fördern wir Zusammenarbeit *durch* digitale Medien (Kommunikation, Kooperation in virtuellen Räumen)?"

**Unique/original:**
- The framing of "Wollen" (will/motivation) as a distinct component of competency is psychologically nuanced.

#### Topic 10: Beurteilung (Assessment)
**Questions:** 6 items on why assess, methods for processes/products/competencies, digital tools for assessment, formative vs. summative, grading/reporting, portfolios.

**Strong research backing:**
- All questions are **foundational to assessment literacy** — critical in competency-based education. Not research-driven per se, but well-established pedagogy.
- Formative vs. summative distinction reflects **black-box assessment design** literature — crucial for learning sciences.
- Competency-oriented assessment aligns with **TPACK's assessment knowledge** and **21st-century assessment frameworks**.
- Digital tools question addresses **technology-enhanced assessment** — a growing field (implicit in Timotheou et al., 2022 on interconnected factors).

**Partial backing:**
- Questions don't address **learning analytics and data-driven assessment** (Michos 2023) — schools increasingly collect granular data on student progress but may lack frameworks for meaningful interpretation. Could sharpen: "Wie nutzen wir digitale Daten (Aktivitätslog, Quiz-Ergebnisse, Portfolio-Analytics) um Lernfortschritt zu verstehen?"
- No question on **assessment bias and equity** — crucial since algorithms and digital tools can amplify disparities. Could add: "Wie stellen wir sicher, dass unsere digitalen Beurteilungsmethoden nicht bestimmte Gruppen von Schüler:innen benachteiligen?"
- No explicit mention of **AI-generated assessment feedback** — Ng et al. (2023) mention this as an emerging competency area. Could add: "Wie gehen wir damit um, dass KI-Systeme Schüler:innen Feedback geben können?"

**Unique/original:**
- Portfolio question is concrete and actionable — reflects pragmatic pedagogy.

#### Topic 11: Arrangements (Teaching/Learning Units)
**Questions:** 4 items on media integration across pedagogical approaches (discovery, cooperative, frontal, workshop, weekly plan, project, blended, individualized), pedagogical models, individual support, feedback timing/methods.

**Strong research backing:**
- Questions on diverse pedagogical arrangements reflect **pedagogical pluralism** — no single "best" approach. This is philosophically sound.
- Media integration across arrangements maps to **SAMR model** (substitution through redefinition across contexts) and **pedagogical knowledge (PK)**.
- Individual support question aligns with **differentiation and personalized learning** — emerging priority in digital contexts.
- Feedback question again ties to **formative assessment and learning sciences**.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions don't address **synchronous vs. asynchronous learning** — a critical distinction in hybrid/remote contexts and increasingly relevant post-COVID. Could add: "Welche Arrangements nutzen synchrone (live) Zusammenarbeit, welche asynchrone (zeitunabhängige)? Wie wählen wir basierend auf pädagogischen Zielen?"
- No question on **accessibility and inclusive design** — crucial for digital arrangements. Could add: "Wie stellen wir sicher, dass digitale Lernumgebungen für alle Schüler:innen zugänglich sind (z.B. Untertitel, Farbkontrast, Bedienung ohne Maus)?"
- No mention of **learning in virtual spaces** — increasingly relevant. Could deepen: "Wie unterscheidet sich effektives Lernen in physischen vs. virtuellen Lernräumen? Welche Medieneinätze passen zu welchen Räumen?"

**Unique/original:**
- The explicit enumeration of multiple pedagogical approaches avoids privileging any one — pedagogically honest.

#### Topic 12: Lernplattformen, Lehrmittel, Tools (Learning Platforms, Teaching Resources, Tools)
**Questions:** 5 items on learning platforms for individual support, digital tools for knowledge/skills, tools for documentation/reflection, creative tools, digital teaching materials.

**Strong research backing:**
- Questions map to **EdTech landscape** — platforms (Moodle, Google Classroom), tools (Padlet, GeoGebra), materials (OER) are all evidence-based when pedagogically embedded.
- Tools for documentation/reflection tie to **portfolio-based learning** and **metacognition** literature.
- Creative tools question aligns with **creative and digital production** as learner outcomes.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions are tool-focused but don't address **tool evaluation frameworks** or **pedagogical alignment**. Schools often pick tools based on popularity or cost, not pedagogy. Could sharpen: "Nach welchen Kriterien wählen wir unsere Lernplattformen und Tools aus? Wie stellen wir sicher, dass die Tools unsere pädagogischen Ziele unterstützen (nicht umgekehrt)?"
- No question on **interoperability and data lock-in** — many tools are proprietary silos. Could add: "Wie stellen wir sicher, dass Schüler:innen-Daten portabel sind und nicht in proprietären Systemen gefangen bleiben?"
- No mention of **AI-powered tutoring and personalization tools** (Ng et al., 2023) — increasingly common (Khan Academy, Duolingo, adaptive learning platforms). Could add: "Wie nutzen wir KI-gestützte Lernplattformen (z.B. adaptive Systeme, automatisches Feedback)? Welche pädagogischen und ethischen Fragen stellen sich?"

**Unique/original:**
- The distinction between platforms (for overall structure) and tools (for specific tasks) is pedagogically useful and avoids conflation.

#### Topic 13: Modullehrplan Medien Informatik (Media & CS Curriculum)
**Questions:** 7 items on curriculum knowledge, building competencies, scheduling, coordination, implementation sites, teaching materials/technology, cross-disciplinary connections.

**Strong research backing:**
- All questions are **curriculum implementation questions** — foundational to education. Reflects **curriculum theory and fidelity of implementation**.
- Timing/scheduling question addresses **curriculum mapping** — essential for systematic coverage.
- Cross-disciplinary connections again echo **integrated curriculum design**.
- Implementation question reflects **adoption of new curricula** — a well-studied change management domain (implicit in Gkrimpizi's school-level barriers).

**Partial backing:**
- Questions assume Lehrplan 21 is *known* and *implemented* but don't surface **why implementation lags** or **barriers to adoption**. Could deepen to map Gkrimpizi: "Welche Hindernisse (personale Unsicherheit, fehlende Zeit, mangelnde Ressourcen, Skepsis) erschweren die Umsetzung des MI-Lehrplans?"
- No question on **evolving curriculum in AI era** — Lehrplan 21 doesn't explicitly cover generative AI. Could ask: "Wie integrieren wir KI-Themen (Funktionsweise, ethische Fragen, Einsatzmöglichkeiten) in den MI-Lehrplan, obwohl dieser noch nicht aktualisiert wurde?"

**Unique/original:**
- The pragmatic focus on "wann arbeiten wir an welchen Kompetenzen?" (when/timing) is implementation-focused.

#### Topic 14: Medienerziehung, Regeln (Media Education & Rules)
**Questions:** 5 items on media use rules, developing critical/autonomous/productive media engagement, incorporating student lifeworld, managing distraction, support resources.

**Strong research backing:**
- Rules and media literacy questions map directly to **critical media education literature** — well-established in German pedagogy (Swertz et al. implicit in Swiss curriculum).
- Critical/autonomous/productive framing reflects **critical literacy and emancipatory pedagogy** — avoiding both tech-skepticism and tech-utopianism.
- Lifeworld incorporation ("Wie nehmen wir die von digitalen Medien geprägte Lebenswelt...im Unterricht auf?") echoes **culturally responsive pedagogy** and **funds of knowledge** — starting with students' actual lives.
- Distraction question is **evidence-based** — research shows multitasking and attention management as challenges in digital learning.
- Support resources question reflects **teacher expertise distribution** — not all teachers are media education specialists.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions don't explicitly address **digital well-being** beyond rules — mental health, social comparison, addiction risk. Could extend: "Wie unterstützen wir Schüler:innen bei digitaler Wohlbefindung (z.B. Schlaf, psychische Gesundheit, Grenzen setzen)?"
- No question on **algorithmic literacy** — students' media consumption is increasingly curated by algorithms (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube). Could add: "Wie helfen wir Schüler:innen zu verstehen, wie Algorithmen ihre Medienkonsum gestalten und beeinflussen?"
- No mention of **generative AI and authenticity** — ChatGPT raises new media education questions (what's real, who's responsible, plagiarism). Could add: "Wie verstehen wir Media Literacy angesichts von KI-generierten Inhalten (Texte, Bilder, Videos)? Wie lehren wir Authentizität und Quellenverifizierung?"

**Unique/original:**
- The distinction between rules (compliance), literacy (understanding), and productive use is pedagogically nuanced.

#### Topic 15: Klassenadministration (Class Administration)
**Questions:** 4 items on lesson preparation, documenting progress, recording absences/contact/grades, information exchange.

**Strong research backing:**
- Questions are **pragmatic and operational** — address daily classroom management. Not research-driven per se, but essential for efficient practice.
- Documentation question ties to **assessment and grading literacy**.
- Information exchange relates to **stakeholder communication and transparency** — important for trust.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions are basic and don't address **administrative burden reduction** — a barrier to digital adoption (implicit in teacher workload literature). Could deepen: "Welche Verwaltungsaufgaben kosten Zeit ohne pädagogischen Mehrwert? Welche könnten durch digitale Tools effizienter gemacht werden?"
- No mention of **data privacy in administrative systems** — Switzerland has strict data protection laws. Could ask: "Wie schützen wir Schüler:innen-Daten in Verwaltungssystemen? Welche Systeme sind datenschutzkonform?"

**Unique/original:**
- The focus on information *exchange* reflects collaborative work — not just individual record-keeping.

### DIMENSION 3: ORGANISATION/STRUKTUREN (Organisation & Structures)

#### Topic 16: Vision (Vision)
**Questions:** 7 items on school identity, learning philosophy, mission statement, future vision, alignment with goals, creating shared energy, digital transformation response.

**Strong research backing:**
- Vision questions are **foundational to organizational development** — schools operate best when purpose is clear and shared. Reflects **purpose-driven change** literature.
- Learning philosophy question ("Welches Bild von Lernen haben wir?") connects to **epistemology and pedagogical worldview** — shape all downstream decisions.
- Mission/values question ("Wie werden diese gelebt?") addresses **cultural alignment** — a critical factor in adoption research (Gkrimpizi et al., 2023).
- Digital transformation response question ("Wie soll unsere Schule auf den digitalen Wandel reagieren?") is the **key strategic question** — ties digital strategy to school identity.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions don't address **vision creation as a participatory process** — research shows co-created visions are more likely to be owned and implemented (Castano-Munoz et al., 2021 on equity and adoption). Could deepen: "Wer war an der Visionsentwicklung beteiligt? Wie stellen wir sicher, dass alle Stimmen gehört werden (auch Skeptiker:innen)?"
- No question on **tension between innovation and stability** — schools must change while maintaining identity. Could add: "Wie balancieren wir zwischen Neuerung und Kontinuität? Was wollen wir bewahren, was wollen wir verändern?"
- No explicit mention of **sustainability** vs. project-based change. Could ask: "Soll unsere digitale Transformation ein zeitlich begrenztes Projekt sein oder eine dauerhafte Entwicklung? Wie stellen wir Nachhaltigkeit sicher?"

**Unique/original:**
- The question on shared energy ("Wie schaffen wir gemeinsame Energie?") is emotionally intelligent and acknowledges the relational dimension of change.

#### Topic 17: Strukturen und Prozesse (Structures and Processes)
**Questions:** 6 items on steering groups/project groups, administrative efficiency, student data management, process efficiency/flexibility, process adaptation, legal frameworks.

**Strong research backing:**
- Steering group question maps to **governance and organizational structure** — critical for coordination (implicit in Timotheou et al., 2022 on interconnected factors).
- Student data management question is **essential for GDPR/Swiss data protection compliance** — legal and ethical imperative.
- Efficiency/flexibility question reflects **organizational agility** — ability to adapt processes without losing coherence (implicit in Gkrimpizi's school-level barriers).
- Legal framework question is **contextually specific to Swiss schools** — shows awareness of regulatory landscape.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions focus on *existence* of structures but not on **effectiveness or participation**. Could sharpen: "Wie effektiv arbeiten unsere Steuergruppen/Projektgruppen? Wie stellen wir sicher, dass alle relevanten Personen eingebunden sind (auch diejenigen, die weniger sichtbar sind)?"
- No question on **external partnerships or networks** — schools increasingly collaborate with external organizations (teacher training, tech vendors, research institutes). Could add: "Mit welchen externen Organisationen (Hochschulen, Behörden, Tech-Firmen, NGOs) arbeiten wir zusammen? Wie gestaltet sich diese Zusammenarbeit?"
- No mention of **horizontal vs. vertical governance** — who decides what. Could ask: "Welche Entscheidungen treffen wir zentral (SL), welche dezentral (Teams)? Wie klar ist diese Rollenverteilung?"

**Unique/original:**
- The question on "bewusst nicht digitalisierte Prozesse" (deliberately non-digital processes) is wise and avoids techno-solutionism.

#### Topic 18: Konzept (Concept/Pedagogy Plan)
**Questions:** 5 items on existence/currency/knowledge of concept, purpose, learning philosophy reflection, participation in development, avoiding "paper tigers."

**Strong research backing:**
- Concept existence question is **foundational** — clear frameworks reduce ambiguity (implicit in curriculum implementation literature).
- Learning philosophy alignment ("Wie wird unser Verständnis des Lernens im Konzept abgebildet?") is sophisticated — ensures pedagogy-first, not technology-first.
- Participation question maps to **co-creation and ownership** — groups own what they help create (implicit in change management literature).
- "Papiertiger" question is pragmatic and reflects **fidelity of implementation** — many schools write plans but don't enact them.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions focus on *pedagogy* but don't explicitly address **technology strategy within the concept**. Could deepen: "Welche Rolle spielen digitale Technologien in unserem pädagogischen Konzept? Sind sie Mittel zum Zweck oder Zweck an sich?"
- No question on **concept evolution and continuous improvement** — concepts shouldn't be static. Could add: "Wie überprüfen wir regelmässig, ob unser pädagogisches Konzept noch aktuell ist? Wie integrieren wir neue Erkenntnisse und Technologien?"

**Unique/original:**
- The distinction between the concept as a living document vs. a paper tiger is pragmatically grounded.

#### Topic 19: Support (Support)
**Questions:** 6 items on technical/pedagogical support roles, hours, job profiles, suitability/strengths, collaboration between roles, support offerings, uptake.

**Strong research backing:**
- Support structure is **foundational to teacher-level and school-level barriers** (Gkrimpizi et al., 2023) — without support, adoption stalls.
- Role clarity question maps to **organizational structure and role clarity** — critical for effective teams.
- Suitability question reflects **human resource fit** — matching person to role increases effectiveness.
- Uptake question addresses **adoption barriers** — good support that's not used is wasted.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions focus on *internal* support but don't address **external support ecosystems** (teacher training institutions, cantons, tech vendors). Could deepen: "Welche externen Supportquellen (Weiterbildungsinstitute, kantonale Stellen, Hersteller-Support) nutzen wir? Wie koordinieren wir interne und externe Unterstützung?"
- No question on **peer learning and professional learning communities (PLCs)** — research shows peer-led learning is highly effective (Castano-Munoz et al., 2021). Could add: "Wie nutzen wir kollegiales Lernen und Peer-Support neben formalem Support-Personal? Wie kultivieren wir Wissensaustausch zwischen Lehrpersonen?"
- No mention of **support for struggling users** — some teachers are resistant or anxious. Could ask: "Wie unterstützen wir Lehrpersonen, die Schwierigkeiten mit digitalen Technologien haben (psychologische Unterstützung, niederschwelliger Zugang zu Hilfe)?"

**Unique/original:**
- The question on what makes support persons suitable for the role reflects human-centered thinking.

#### Topic 20: Führung (Leadership)
**Questions:** 8 items on leadership modeling, role/expertise, staff expertise distribution, supporting support staff, school board role, participation/information of staff, autonomy/standards balance, professional development prioritization.

**Strong research backing:**
- Modeling question ("Wo übernimmt die SL eine Vorbildfunktion?") maps directly to **digital leadership literature** — leaders must demonstrate competence and commitment to normalize digital practice.
- Expertise identification question ("Wer hat sonst relevante Expertise?") reflects **distributed leadership** — effective schools don't rely on single leaders but distribute expertise.
- Supporting support staff question shows **awareness of second-order support** — support people themselves need support.
- Autonomy/standards balance ("Freiheit/Autonomie und Verbindlichkeit/Standards") is philosophically nuanced — reflects tension between teacher autonomy and school coherence.
- Staff participation in change is a **critical success factor** (Gkrimpizi et al., 2023) — people resist change they don't co-create.

**Partial backing:**
- Questions don't address **change management strategies and resistance** — some staff will resist. Could sharpen: "Welche Strategien nutzen wir, um mit Widerständen und Skeptizismus produktiv umzugehen? Wie verwandeln wir Kritiker:innen in konstruktive Partner:innen?"
- No question on **distributed leadership development** — how to build leadership capacity beyond the formal leader. Could add: "Wie entwickeln wir Führungskompetenzen bei Lehrpersonen und Support-Personal? Wer übernimmt Verantwortung für Teilbereiche der Transformation?"

### Gap 4: Digital Equity and the Three-Level Digital Divide

**Research background:**
Warschauer (2004) defines digital divide in three levels: (1) access to devices, (2) digital skills, (3) meaningful outcomes. van de Werfhorst (2022) extends this, showing that access alone doesn't close gaps — underlying inequalities persist.

**Where missing from Kompass:**
- Topic 7 (Parent Participation) mentions multilingual families but not explicitly equity
- Topic 29 (Devices) asks about quantity but not about ensuring equitable access for all learners
- No overarching reflection on digital equity as systemic issue

**Why it matters:**
- Digital divide is increasing post-COVID; privileged families have better home tech than disadvantaged ones
- Schools can amplify or reduce inequality depending on how they approach digital tools
- Switzerland is diverse; attention to equity is important for social cohesion

### Gap 5: Professional Learning Communities and Peer Learning Networks

**Research background:**
Castano-Munoz et al. (2021) show that peer-led professional learning and teacher networks outperform top-down training. Maya Jariego (2023) emphasizes social network analysis in school contexts. Teacher collaboration and shared learning are critical for adoption.

**Where missing from Kompass:**
- Topic 19 (Support) focuses on formal support roles, not peer learning
- Topic 24 (Cooperation) asks about cooperation generally, not specifically about professional learning
- Topic 25 (Knowledge Management) addresses knowledge sharing but not peer learning communities (PLCs)

**Why it matters:**
- Teachers learn best from other teachers; peer leadership is more credible than top-down training
- PLCs create sustainable culture change, not one-time training
- Peer networks reduce isolation and increase innovation diffusion

### Gap 6: Teacher Digital Mindset vs. Skills

**Research background:**
Pietsch (2024) distinguishes **digital mindset** (curiosity, openness, willingness to learn) from **digital skills** (ability to use tools). Mindset predicts long-term adoption better than skills; someone with low skills but high mindset will learn, but someone with high skills but low mindset will resist.

**Where missing from Kompass:**
- Topic 6 (Mindsets) touches on this implicitly but not explicitly named
- Topic 5 (Application Skills) focuses on skills, not mindset
- No reflection on mindset as *distinct* from skills

**Why it matters:**
- Skills training alone doesn't drive transformation; mindset is the bottleneck
- Schools can develop mindset through culture, leadership modeling, and psychological safety
- Understanding this distinction changes how schools approach professional development

**Suggested questions to add (German):**

1. **Under "Haltungen" (Mindsets — Topic 6):**
   - "Unterscheiden wir zwischen digitalen Kompetenzen (Fähigkeiten) und digitaler Haltung (Offenheit, Lernwilligkeit)? Wo sind unsere Stärken und Lücken?"
   - "Wer in unserem Team hat eine starke digitale Haltung auch mit niedrigem Skillniveau? Wie unterstützen wir ihr Lernen?"

2. **Under "Personalentwicklung" (as sub-topic of Support — Topic 19):**
   - "Wie entwickeln wir digitale Haltungen (nicht nur Skills)? Welche Praktiken fördern Neugier und Lernwilligkeit (Modellierung, psychologische Sicherheit, Experimentieren)?"

### Gap 7: School Networks and External Collaboration for Digital Innovation

**Why it matters:**

- Cantons/districts set policy constraints; schools need to understand and navigate these

### Gap 8: Assessment and Authenticity in AI Era

**Research background:**
Emerging research (Ng et al. 2023, Tenberga et al. 2024) shows AI challenging traditional assessment assumptions. What counts as "authentic" work when students use AI? How do we assess what students have *learned* vs. what AI produced?

**Where missing from Kompass:**
- Topic 10 (Assessment) doesn't explicitly address AI-mediated assessment
- No reflection on changing epistemology of authorship/authenticity

**Why it matters:**
- ChatGPT can write essays, code, and math solutions; assessment models built on "show your work" are disrupted
- Schools need new frameworks for authenticity, collaboration, and appropriate AI use
- This is urgent and changing fast

**Suggested questions to add (German):**

## Part C: AI as a New Topic

### Proposed Topic: "Künstliche Intelligenz" (AI)

#### Positioning in Framework

**Suggested placement:**
- **Primary location:** Separate 6th topic under Dimension 1 (Personen/Kompetenzen — People & Skills), *after* Topic 6 (Haltungen/Mindsets) and *before* Topic 7 (Zusammenarbeit mit Eltern)
  - Rationale: AI is fundamentally about people's competence, mindset, and how they work together. It's less about infrastructure (that's Topic 29–35) and more about how teachers and learners understand and use AI.

- **Alternative placements:**
  - As a cross-cutting theme integrated into multiple dimensions (Mediendidaktik, Beurteilung, Lernplattformen, Sicherheit)
  - As a sub-topic under "Mediendidaktik" (Topic 4) — AI-generated and AI-enhanced media

**Rationale for primary location under People & Skills:**
- AI is not primarily a tool (infrastructure) but a **competency and mindset issue**
- Teachers first need to *understand* AI before they can teach it or teach *with* it
- AI raises ethical, epistemological questions that belong under "Haltungen" (mindsets)
- Growth: as AI adoption matures, this topic may move toward infrastructure; today it's premature competency issue

---

#### Cross-Links to Existing Topics

**Strong connections:**
- **Topic 6 (Haltungen/Mindsets):** How teachers think about AI's role in education
- **Topic 4 (Mediendidaktik):** Teaching *with* AI tools; AI-generated media as pedagogical material
- **Topic 5 (Anwendungskompetenzen):** Skills teachers need to use AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, etc.)
- **Topic 9 (Interdisciplinary Skills/4Ks):** AI literacy as 21st-century competency for students
- **Topic 10 (Beurteilung/Assessment):** Assessment in AI era; authenticity and authorship
- **Topic 14 (Medienerziehung):** Critical AI literacy — understanding how AI systems work, biases, limitations
- **Topic 31 (Software/Services):** Many tools now embed AI; which ones do we use?
- **Topic 32 (Sicherheit/Security):** AI-enabled threats (deepfakes, adversarial attacks) and privacy (data used to train models)
- **Topic 2 (Fachkompetenz Medien Informatik):** CS curriculum should include AI/machine learning concepts

**Moderate connections:**
- **Topic 8 (Lernkultur):** How AI changes teaching/learning relationship
- **Topic 20 (Führung/Leadership):** Leadership role in setting AI policy
- **Topic 28 (Dynamiken und Emotionen):** Teacher anxiety and resistance to AI

#### Reflection Questions (7 questions in German)

1. **"Welches Verständnis haben wir von künstlicher Intelligenz? Was können KI-Systeme, was nicht? Welche Chancen und Grenzen sehen wir?"**
   - Rationale: Builds shared understanding; counters both techno-optimism and techno-skepticism

2. **"Wie sollen Schüler:innen über KI lernen (Funktionsweise, Stärken, Schwächen, Ethik) um informierte Nutzer:innen und kritische Denker:innen zu werden?"**
   - Rationale: AI literacy as learning outcome; maps to Topic 14 (critical media literacy) and Topic 9 (21st-century skills)

3. **"Welche KI-Tools (z.B. ChatGPT, Bild-Generatoren, adaptive Lernplattformen) nutzen oder könnten wir nutzen? Für welche pädagogischen Zwecke? Welche Vorbehalte haben wir?"**
   - Rationale: Practical question about tool adoption; surfaces both opportunities and concerns

4. **"Wie gehen wir damit um, dass Schüler:innen KI-Tools bei Hausaufgaben und Arbeiten nutzen können? Wie definieren wir ethische und uneethische Nutzung? Wo sind die Grenzen zwischen Unterstützung und Betrug?"**
   - Rationale: Assessment integrity in AI era; links to Topic 10 (Beurteilung) and Topic 34 (Rechtliche Aspekte)

5. **"Wie transparently und ethically gehen wir mit KI-Systemen um (z.B. Datenschutz, Bias, Überwachung, Urheberrecht von generierten Inhalten)?"**
   - Rationale: AI ethics; links to Topic 32 (Security) and Topic 34 (Legal aspects); addresses growing concerns about surveillance and fairness

6. **"Welche Kompetenzen im Umgang mit KI brauchen Lehrpersonen? Wo sehen wir Qualifizierungsbedarf? Wie unterstützen wir Lehrpersonen, die unsicher sind?"**
   - Rationale: Teacher development; maps to Topic 2 (Professional skills) and Topic 5 (Application skills); acknowledges anxiety

7. **"Wie verändert KI die Rolle von Lehrpersonen und Schüler:innen? Was sind neue Chancen für Personalisierung und Unterstützung? Welche Aspekte der menschlichen Beziehung werden unverzichtbar?"**
   - Rationale: Epistemological reflection on teaching/learning; links to Topic 8 (Learning culture) and Topic 6 (Mindsets); preserves human-centered pedagogy

#### Brief Illustration Description

**Suggested visual:**
A design showing **human and AI working together in complementary ways:**
- Left side: Human skills (empathy, creativity, context, moral judgment, relationship)
- Center: Overlap/collaboration zone (AI augments human capability)
- Right side: AI strengths (speed, pattern recognition, tireless processing, data analysis)
- Bottom: Question mark or open dialogue — representing ongoing negotiation of roles

**Why this framing:**
- Avoids both dystopian (AI replaces humans) and utopian (AI solves everything) narratives
- Shows genuine collaboration, not zero-sum competition
- Invites reflection on what's uniquely human

#### Rationale Connecting to Research

**Research foundations:**

1. **Ng et al. (2023, 345 citations):** Integrated AI into DigCompEdu, showing AI literacy as distinct competency category. Our topic directly addresses "AI competency" as teacher/student outcome.

2. **Chiu et al. (2024, 69 citations):** Found that reflection tools supporting autonomy, competence, and relatedness drive adoption. Our reflection questions offer *autonomy* (choice in how to engage with AI), *competence* (understanding what AI can/can't do), and *relatedness* (seeing AI as tool for human connection, not replacement).

3. **Tenberga et al. (2024):** Document rapid changes in teacher roles, assessment, and learning design due to generative AI. Our questions directly surface these changes and invite reflection.

4. **Pietsch (2024):** Emphasizes digital mindset (not just skills) as driver of adoption. Questions 1, 6, 7 focus on mindset and understanding, not just tool use.

5. **Gkrimpizi et al. (2023, 149 citations):** Identify teacher-level barriers to adoption. AI anxiety is a growing teacher-level barrier; our topic creates space for psychological safety and sense-making.

6. **Warschauer (2004); van de Werfhorst (2022):** Address digital equity and three levels of divide. Question 4 (ethics, authorship, access) touches on equity dimensions of AI adoption.

## Summary & Recommendations

1. **Strong alignment across 35 topics:** The Kompass reflects solid research foundations (TPACK, Self-Determination Theory, change management, digital equity awareness). Questions are generally thought-provoking and non-judgmental.
